# Contractor Vetting Options

Zayith decision needed: choose soft launch speed or stricter verified trust before routing real homeowners.

## Option A — Soft launch checkbox path

Use when speed matters and initial volume is founder-supervised.

### Approval standard
- Contractor signs up with business name, contact info, service ZIPs, service categories, license field, and legal/payment consents.
- Founder/Jarvis performs plausibility review: local HVAC fit, reachable phone/email, no obvious spam, service area matches Sacramento launch.
- Contractor can be approved for limited launch routing without automated CSLB/insurance verification.

### Guardrails
- Route low initial volume only.
- Keep no more than 3 contractors per homeowner request.
- Suspend quickly on complaints, unreachable contact info, fee dispute, safety concern, or bad-faith quote behavior.
- Manual review any change order, dispute, or abnormal pricing.

### Pros
- Fastest Monday launch path.
- No new vendor/API dependency.
- Good fit for concierge/manual first customers.

### Cons
- Higher trust risk.
- More founder/Jarvis manual oversight.
- Less defensible if homeowner asks what “vetted” means.

### Suggested copy if chosen
“Contractors are manually reviewed for local fit, service coverage, signup information, and agreement to NeighborlyWork’s launch standards before receiving homeowner requests.”

## Option B — Verified CSLB + insurance path

Use when stronger trust positioning matters more than launch speed.

### Approval standard
- Contractor must provide CSLB license number.
- Founder/Jarvis verifies license status through CSLB public lookup before approval.
- Contractor must provide current insurance proof or attest insurance with follow-up verification before receiving live leads.
- Approval notes should capture verifier, date, CSLB status, insurance evidence status, and any limitations.

### Guardrails
- No routing until verification complete.
- Re-check on complaints, expired evidence, ownership mismatch, or material business-name mismatch.
- Store only non-sensitive verification notes unless a proper document storage/privacy path is approved.

### Pros
- Stronger “vetted” claim.
- Better homeowner trust.
- Lower contractor-quality risk.

### Cons
- Slower launch.
- More manual effort.
- May require document handling policy and storage decision.

### Suggested copy if chosen
“Contractors are reviewed for local service fit and must pass license and insurance verification before receiving live homeowner requests.”

## Recommendation
For Monday: use Option A only if Zayith is comfortable with a concierge soft launch and conservative manual routing. Use Option B before broader public traffic or paid acquisition.
